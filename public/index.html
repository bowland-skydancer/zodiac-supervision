About This App
What This Is — and What It Isn't
This is a structured reflective thinking aid for practising coaches who already have human supervision. It extends the supervisory space between sessions. It is not supervision.
What it does

The app uses twelve lenses drawn from the Chinese zodiac — each representing a distinct supervisory perspective — to help coaches think more broadly about their cases. In group mode, each participant holds one lens and generates questions from it. In self-supervision mode, an AI reads your case description and selects the most relevant lenses, tailoring the questions to your specific situation.

The design deliberately resists resolution. The lenses generate questions, not answers. The aim is to widen inquiry, not close it down.

Honest limitations

No relational presence. Supervision works through the quality of a real relationship — presence, attunement, the supervisor's genuine response to what they're hearing. This app can simulate curiosity but not experience it. It has no stake in the relationship, no felt sense, no counter-transference to notice and work with.

No parallel process. One of supervision's most powerful phenomena — where the dynamics between coach and client are unconsciously re-enacted in the supervision relationship — cannot occur here. An AI cannot be recruited into a parallel process, and therefore cannot name one.

No professional accountability. When a case involves safeguarding concerns, risk of harm, or complex ethical edges, supervision requires judgment grounded in professional responsibility. This app has no registration, no duty of care in any legally meaningful sense. It can surface a question; it cannot hold the weight of the responsibility.

The risk of false closure. Well-formed questions and tidy frameworks can create an illusion of having been supervised. This app includes a specific prompt designed to counteract this — but the risk remains. If the process feels complete, treat that as data, not as a signal to stop.

What it can honestly offer

Available between sessions. Human supervision is time-bounded. This app is available whenever a case is pressing — at 11pm before a difficult session, or immediately after one.

Non-judgmental space. Some coaches find it easier to surface things they'd feel exposed bringing to a human supervisor — at least initially. This app can serve as a thinking-through space before a human conversation.

Multiple perspectives simultaneously. The lens structure deliberately brings competing angles to bear on a single case, reducing the risk of tunnel vision.

Widening access. Regular human supervision is expensive and not always accessible. Used honestly, this app can support coaches who would otherwise have no reflective space at all.

A note on confidentiality

In self-supervision mode, the text you write is sent to an AI language model for processing. Before submitting, please anonymise all client details — remove names, identifying roles, organisations, and any information that could identify the person you are writing about. Your clients have not consented to their situation being processed by an AI system.

When to use human supervision instead

If your case involves a safeguarding concern, a risk of harm to any person, a serious ethical dilemma, or significant distress in yourself as a coach — please bring it to a qualified human supervisor. This app will signal that too, but you don't need the app to tell you: if the stakes feel high, take it to a person.
